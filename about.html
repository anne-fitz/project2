Out of Bounds offers a unique user experience that contrasts with typical websites by challenging the way we interact with online content. By using a lens to highlight the limits of what one sees online, this website emphasizes how much control standard web interfaces exert over the experience of the site. This project challenges that control by promoting user autonomy and critiquing our digital lack of freedom. It also serves as a reflection on how technology and other lenses–both literally and figuratively–limit our perception of our world.

The ideal user for this site is someone interested in interactive digital experiences and looking for a way to challenge their own thinking of how they navigate the web. They will uncover a playful site that explores these themes.

The idea of this website came from my own experiences. On my walk to class, I noticed that everyone around me was glued to their phones, seemingly unaware of their surroundings. It made me think how much they must miss by just focusing on the rectangle in front of them. This thought evolved and I considered the ways in which distorted viewpoints lead to people missing the whole picture: how we only see curated images online and miss people's true realities; how rose-colored glasses in a relationship can shield us from red flags; how tunnel vision on bigger life milestones can cause us to not appreciate the smaller moments of life. Similarly, websites control what you see and experience as well: you can only click on what the designer created. Through this website, I wanted to explore the idea of control and perception and craft an experience where users can actively uncover content for themselves. The user uses lenses to display or hide different parts of the page, able to see both through a distorted view and the entire picture.

I consider there to be many conceptual limitations to this project. It is abstract and the metaphor is not quite intuitive. I struggled with conceptualizing the whole idea myself and then implementing it in code. I think that the website could be clearer and the have more nuanced interactivity to further highlight the limitations of our current web experiences.

In the next iteration of this website, I would love to fix the aesthetic of it. I used images from Pinterest but I would love to make my own content that better aligns with the meaning and is more cohesive. Additionally, I’d reconsider the metaphor and ensure that it communicates my vision of letting the user uncover content on their own and understand how different perspectives sometimes shield you from the whole picture. Lastly, I would want to add mobile responsiveness where it says it's a desktop only website when opened on mobile, as I ran out of time for that.
